What Is Pneumonia?

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. It can cause symptoms like cough, fever, shortness of breath, and chest pain.

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Bacterial pneumonia: Commonly caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae.
  • Viral pneumonia: Often caused by influenza viruses.
  • Fungal pneumonia: Associated with certain fungi.
  • Risk factors: Age, weakened immune system, smoking, and chronic lung conditions.

Common Symptoms

  • Cough with mucus or phlegm
  • Fever, often high-grade with chills
  • Fast breathing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain while coughing
  • Fast heartbeat
  • Feeling very tired or weak
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Loss of appetite
  • Body pain

Diagnosis and Treatment

Diagnosis involves physical exams, chest X-rays, and lab tests. Treatment options include antibiotics, antivirals, and supportive care.

Prevention

Prevent pneumonia by getting vaccinated (especially for flu and pneumococcal infections), practicing good hygiene, and avoiding smoking.
